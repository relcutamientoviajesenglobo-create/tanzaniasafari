Tanzania is one of the safest safari destinations in Africa. National parks are well-managed by TANAPA, guides are certified professionals and the tourism infrastructure is developed to ensure a safe experience. However, you are in the wild and it's important to know the basic rules.

## Vehicle Rules

These are the fundamental rules every visitor must follow during game drives:

1. **Never exit the vehicle** without your guide's permission
2. **Don't stand on seats** when the roof is open
3. **Don't extend arms or legs** through windows
4. **Keep your voice low** to avoid startling animals
5. **Never feed** any animal under any circumstances
6. **Don't throw litter** inside the park

## Health & Prevention

### Malaria
Tanzania is endemic for malaria. Preventive measures include:
- Antimalarial prophylaxis prescribed by your doctor
- DEET 30%+ repellent applied at dusk and dawn
- Long-sleeved clothing in light colors during twilight hours
- Always sleep under a mosquito net

### Water & Food
- Drink only bottled or purified water
- Food at lodges and camps meets international hygiene standards

### Sun & Heat
- Use SPF 50+ sunscreen and reapply every 2-3 hours
- Stay hydrated with at least 2 liters of water daily

## Travel Insurance

Travel insurance with medical and evacuation coverage is absolutely recommended. Air medical evacuations from the parks can cost thousands of dollars without insurance.
