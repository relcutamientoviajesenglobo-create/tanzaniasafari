Packing for a Tanzania safari requires planning. The African savanna has variable weather conditions: cold mornings, hot afternoons, dust, intense sun and possible rain. This guide covers everything you need to pack for the ultimate adventure.

## Essential Clothing

The main rule is to wear neutral, natural colors. Animals don't distinguish these colors and insects are less attracted to earth tones.

### Upper Layers
- 3-4 lightweight long-sleeve shirts (sun protection)
- 2 short-sleeve shirts for warm afternoons
- 1 fleece or polar jacket for cold mornings (can drop to 10°C)
- 1 lightweight packable rain jacket

### Lower Layers
- 2-3 convertible pants (that convert to shorts)
- 1 pair of shorts for the lodge
- Quick-dry breathable underwear

### Footwear
- Lightweight closed-toe hiking shoes (most important)
- Sandals for the lodge
- Merino wool socks (3-4 pairs)

## Camera Gear

Wildlife photography is a fundamental part of safari.

- Camera with telephoto lens (minimum 200mm, ideal 100-400mm)
- Extra memory cards (minimum 128GB total)
- Additional charged batteries
- Bean bag for stabilizing camera on the vehicle
- Dust-proof protective bag

## Protection & Health

- SPF 50+ waterproof sunscreen
- Insect repellent with 30%+ DEET
- Malaria prophylaxis (consult your doctor)
- Basic first aid kit
- Personal medications
- Hand sanitizer

## What NOT to Bring

- Bright colored clothing (especially blue, which attracts tsetse flies)
- White clothing (gets dirty immediately with dust)
- Scented perfumes or lotions (attract insects)
- Valuable jewelry
- Drones (prohibited in national parks)
- Excess luggage (bush planes have 15kg soft bag limit)
