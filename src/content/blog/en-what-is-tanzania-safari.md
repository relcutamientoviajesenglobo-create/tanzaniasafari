A Tanzania safari is an expedition to observe African wildlife in its natural habitat. Tanzania harbors some of the richest ecosystems on the planet, including the Serengeti, the Ngorongoro Crater and more than 16 national parks protecting millions of animals.

## How Does a Safari Work?

A typical safari consists of game drives in specially adapted 4x4 vehicles, led by a professional driver-guide with expert knowledge of wildlife and terrain. Vehicles have pop-up roofs allowing observation and photography from an elevated 360-degree vantage point.

### A Typical Safari Day

A safari day starts early, usually before dawn (5:30-6:00 AM), when animals are most active and the light is ideal for photography. The morning game drive lasts 3-5 hours.

After lunch at the lodge or a bush picnic, a second game drive takes place in the afternoon (3:30-6:30 PM), coinciding with golden hour photography and evening predator activity.

## The Big Five

1. **African Lion**: the apex predator of the savanna. Tanzania has one of Africa's largest lion populations, with over 15,000 individuals.
2. **Leopard**: the most elusive of the Big Five. Found in all Tanzania parks but spotting one requires patience and an experienced guide.
3. **African Elephant**: the largest land animal on Earth. Tarangire has one of the largest elephant concentrations in East Africa.
4. **Cape Buffalo**: lives in large herds that can exceed 1,000 individuals. Common in Ngorongoro and Serengeti.
5. **Black Rhinoceros**: the hardest to spot due to its critically endangered status. Ngorongoro Crater is one of the best places in the world to see them.

## Main National Parks

### Serengeti National Park
Africa's most famous park, with 14,763 km² of savanna. Home to the Great Migration and the highest density of predators on the continent.

### Ngorongoro Conservation Area
The world's largest volcanic crater (20 km diameter) functions as a closed ecosystem with extraordinary wildlife concentration. A UNESCO World Heritage Site.

### Tarangire National Park
Famous for enormous elephant herds (up to 3,000 in dry season) and iconic ancient baobab trees.

## Types of Safari

- **Classic safari (game drive)**: the most popular format, in 4x4 vehicles
- **Photography safari**: focused on professional photography with extended observation times
- **Walking safari**: guided walks with armed rangers for close bush experience
- **Night safari**: nocturnal drives to observe night wildlife like leopards and hyenas
- **Balloon safari**: sunrise flights over the Serengeti
