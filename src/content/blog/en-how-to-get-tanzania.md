Tanzania has several international airports receiving flights from around the world. The main gateway for safaris is Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO), located between Arusha and Moshi, just a short drive from the major northern national parks.

## Main Airports

### Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO)
The most convenient airport for northern circuit safaris (Serengeti, Ngorongoro, Tarangire, Lake Manyara). Located 46 km from Arusha, the safari capital. Receives direct flights from Amsterdam (KLM), Istanbul (Turkish Airlines), Doha (Qatar Airways) and Nairobi (Kenya Airways).

### Julius Nyerere International Airport (DAR)
Located in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania's largest city. Best option for southern circuit safaris (Selous/Nyerere, Ruaha) and trips to Zanzibar. Has more international connections than Kilimanjaro.

## Entry Requirements

### Visa
Most nationalities require a visa to enter Tanzania:
- **e-Visa**: apply online at immigration.go.tz ($50 USD single entry, $100 USD multiple). Processing takes 3-10 business days.
- **Visa on Arrival**: available at major airports ($50 USD). Long queues possible.
- **Required documents**: passport valid for at least 6 months, proof of accommodation and return flight.

### Vaccinations
- **Yellow fever**: mandatory if coming from an endemic country
- **Recommended**: hepatitis A and B, typhoid, tetanus
- **Malaria**: no vaccine exists, but prophylaxis is recommended (consult your doctor)

## Transport from Airport

Most safari operators include airport transfers from Kilimanjaro to your Arusha hotel (45 minutes) or directly to the first safari lodge. Bush flights connect Arusha with airstrips inside the Serengeti (1 hour), Ngorongoro, Tarangire and other parks.

### Driving Distances from Arusha
- **Tarangire**: 120 km (2 hours)
- **Lake Manyara**: 130 km (2.5 hours)
- **Ngorongoro**: 190 km (3.5 hours)
- **Serengeti (Seronera)**: 335 km (7-8 hours)
