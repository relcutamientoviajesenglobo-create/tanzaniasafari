Tanzania is one of the world's top safari destinations, and one of the most common questions is when to visit. The answer depends on what you want to see: the Great Migration, the Big Five, lush green landscapes, or simply the best weather possible.

## Season Overview

Tanzania has two dry seasons and two wet seasons that determine wildlife patterns across the entire Serengeti-Mara ecosystem.

### Long Dry Season (June — October)

This is peak safari season and the most popular time for visitors. The weather is dry and cool, short vegetation makes wildlife spotting easier, and herds concentrate near rivers and waterholes. It's the ideal time for the Mara River crossings during the Great Migration.

Temperatures range from 15°C at night to 28°C during the day. Clear skies provide the best conditions for wildlife photography.

### Short Rains (November — December)

This shoulder season offers a perfect balance between good weather and more accessible prices. Rains are usually brief afternoon showers, and the landscape transforms into a vibrant green savanna. Excellent for photography with dramatic skies.

### Long Rains (March — May)

This is low season with the lowest prices. Rainfall can be intense and some roads become difficult. However, it's the best time for migratory bird watching and the landscape is at its greenest.

### Short Dry Season (January — February)

This is calving season in the southern Serengeti. Thousands of wildebeest give birth on the Ndutu plains, attracting predators like lions, cheetahs, and hyenas. It's spectacular for safari and relatively less crowded than the long dry season.

## Best Time by National Park

Each national park has its own optimal conditions:

- **Serengeti**: June to October for migration; January-February for calving
- **Ngorongoro**: Year-round thanks to its closed ecosystem; June-September for best weather
- **Tarangire**: June to October when elephants concentrate along the river
- **Lake Manyara**: June to October for flamingos and concentrated wildlife
- **Selous/Nyerere**: June to November; partially closes during rainy season

## Tips for Choosing Your Date

If your priority is seeing the Great Migration with the Mara River crossings, plan your trip between July and September. If you prefer fewer crowds and lower prices, November-December or January-February are excellent options with great wildlife activity.

Book at least 6 months in advance for peak season, as the best lodges and camps fill up quickly.
