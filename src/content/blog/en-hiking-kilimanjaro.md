Mount Kilimanjaro, at 5,895 meters above sea level, is Africa's highest mountain and the world's tallest freestanding mountain. Located in northeastern Tanzania near the Kenyan border, it is one of the most popular trekking objectives on the planet.

## Main Routes

### Marangu Route (5-6 days)
Known as the "Coca-Cola Route," it's the only route with hut accommodation instead of tents. Most popular and shortest, which paradoxically makes it harder due to insufficient acclimatization time.
- **Difficulty**: Moderate
- **Success rate**: 45-50% (5 days), 65% (6 days)

### Machame Route (6-7 days)
The "Whiskey Route" is most popular among experienced hikers. Offers varied scenery and superior acclimatization with "hike high, sleep low" strategy.
- **Difficulty**: Moderate-High
- **Success rate**: 73-78%

### Lemosho Route (7-8 days)
Considered the best route for its combination of spectacular scenery, good acclimatization and fewer hikers in the early days.
- **Difficulty**: Moderate
- **Success rate**: 85%+

## Physical Preparation

No technical mountaineering skills required, but good physical preparation is essential:
- Start a training program at least 3 months before
- Focus on cardiovascular endurance
- Include leg and core strength training
- Practice loaded hiking on inclined terrain

## Altitude Sickness (AMS)

Acute mountain sickness is the main challenge. Keys to prevention:
- Choose a route of at least 7 days
- Walk slowly ("pole pole" in Swahili)
- Stay hydrated (3-4 liters daily)
- Consider preventive medication (Acetazolamide/Diamox)

## Combining Kilimanjaro + Safari

Many travelers combine Kilimanjaro climbing with a safari. The recommended sequence is Kilimanjaro first, then safari, allowing rest and recovery while enjoying game drives.
