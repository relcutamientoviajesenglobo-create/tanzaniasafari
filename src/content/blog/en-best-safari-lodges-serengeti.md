Choosing where to stay on a Serengeti safari is almost as important as the safari itself. Your accommodation sets the tone for your entire experience — from waking up to lions roaring outside your tent to watching the sunset from an infinity pool overlooking the endless plains.

## Luxury Lodges vs Tented Camps

The Serengeti offers two distinct accommodation styles, each with its own charm.

### Safari Lodges

Permanent lodges are built into the landscape using local materials — stone, hardwood and thatch. They typically offer spacious rooms, en-suite bathrooms with hot water, restaurants serving international cuisine, swimming pools and often a spa. The best lodges blend seamlessly into the environment whilst providing five-star comfort.

**Top luxury lodges:**
- **Four Seasons Safari Lodge Serengeti** — The only international luxury brand in the Serengeti. Infinity pool overlooking a watering hole, world-class dining and impeccable service. From $1,200 per night.
- **Singita Grumeti** — Consistently ranked among Africa's finest. Three exclusive properties across 350,000 acres of private concession. Exceptional wine cellar and conservation programme. From $2,000 per night.
- **&Beyond Serengeti Under Canvas** — Mobile luxury that follows the Great Migration. Your camp moves with the herds, ensuring you are always in the heart of the action.

### Tented Camps

Luxury tented camps offer an authentic bush experience without sacrificing comfort. Canvas walls let you hear every sound of the African night — from hyena calls to the rustle of elephants passing nearby. Most tents feature proper beds with quality linens, en-suite bathrooms with flush toilets and hot showers, and private verandas.

**Top tented camps:**
- **Asilia Namiri Plains** — Located in the eastern Serengeti, famous for cheetah sightings. Intimate camp with just 10 tents. From $750 per night.
- **Sanctuary Kichakani Serengeti Camp** — A mobile camp that follows the migration. Elegant tents with campaign-style furnishings. From $600 per night.
- **Lemala Ewanjan** — Set in the heart of the Seronera Valley, the Serengeti's richest wildlife area. Walking distance to the river crossings. From $550 per night.

## What to Expect from Safari Accommodation

### Meals and Dining

Most safari lodges operate on a full-board or all-inclusive basis. Expect:
- **Bush breakfast** — cooked to order at a scenic spot during morning game drives
- **Lunch** — buffet-style at the lodge or a packed picnic lunch in the bush
- **Sundowner drinks** — cocktails at a scenic viewpoint as the sun sets
- **Dinner** — multi-course meals, often served under the stars with a campfire

### Wildlife at Your Doorstep

One of the magical aspects of Serengeti accommodation is the wildlife that visits your camp. Elephants walking past your tent, hippos grazing on the lodge lawns, and leopards lounging in nearby trees are common occurrences. Lodges with waterholes — like Four Seasons — offer wildlife viewing without leaving the property.

### Connectivity and Power

Most lodges offer solar-powered electricity and limited Wi-Fi in common areas. Mobile signal is patchy in the Serengeti. Embrace the digital detox — it is part of the experience.

## Budget-Friendly Options

Not every safari lodge costs thousands. Excellent mid-range and budget options include:

- **Serengeti Serena Safari Lodge** — A classic mid-range option with stunning Maasai-inspired architecture. Rooms from $350 per night.
- **Serengeti Sopa Lodge** — Perched on the Nyamara Hills with panoramic views. Good value at $250-350 per night.
- **Ikoma Bush Camp** — An affordable tented camp near the western corridor. From $150 per night, ideal for budget-conscious travellers.
- **Public campsites** — For the adventurous, TANAPA operates public campsites within the park from $30 per person per night. Basic facilities but an unforgettable experience.

## How to Choose the Right Lodge

Consider these factors:

1. **Location within the Serengeti** — The central Seronera area has year-round wildlife. The western corridor and northern Serengeti are best during migration season (June-October).
2. **Your budget** — Prices range from $30 (camping) to $3,000+ (ultra-luxury). Determine your comfort level and allocate accordingly.
3. **Travel dates** — Peak season (July-October) commands premium rates. The green season (March-May) offers significant discounts and fewer tourists.
4. **Group size** — Intimate camps with 6-10 tents offer exclusivity. Larger lodges with 50+ rooms provide more facilities and social atmosphere.
5. **Special interests** — Photographers should choose camps near river crossings. Families benefit from lodges with pools and family suites. Honeymooners will love private bush dinners and spa treatments.
